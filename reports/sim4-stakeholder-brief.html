Six Key Findings

Evidence from the multi-agent simulation across 8 rounds and 14 stakeholder types.

01
Retention Revolution
Brain drain falls from 1,200 to 400/year. Returnees triple from 105 to 280/year. The existing workforce is kept and expanded without adding a single new graduate.
02
Contract Abolition
The single biggest retention barrier is removed. Permanent posts expand by +10%/year. Contract doctors drop from 14,400 to zero. Career certainty is fully restored.
03
Salary Competitiveness
Public salary ratio rises from 0.47 to 0.58, approaching private-sector competitiveness. On-call and hazard pay are restored. The 25% increase is the largest in a decade.
04
Working Conditions Transformed
Average workload falls from 72 to 52 hours/week. 3-month deployment notice, rural incentives, and workload caps are implemented across all health districts.
05
Health Services Commission
Full establishment by 2028 breaks the MOHE-JPA-MOH silos. End-to-end workforce planning becomes institutionalized, not politicized.
06
Student Interest Rises
Medicine regains prestige. Interest index climbs from 1.88 to 2.44 as graduates see secure, dignified career pathways — even with flat intake.

Conclusion

Summary judgment for stakeholders and decision-makers.

Retention-Only Works

Malaysia achieves 2.50 doctors/1,000 through retention alone — no intake increase required. A fundamentally different path from SIM-3.

Contract Abolition is Transformative

Zero contract doctors by Round 5. Permanent posts expand +10%/year. Brain drain halves. Returnees triple.

Working Conditions Drive Retention

Workload drops from 72 to 52 hours/week. Overcrowding falls 27%. The profession becomes sustainable and dignified.

Slower Than SIM-3, But Safer

Absolute growth is slower than intake-driven expansion. But the risk of boom-bust cycles is eliminated entirely.

Risk Assessment

Slower absolute growth than SIM-3. If population grows faster than expected or NCD burden accelerates, the static intake of 4,820 may still leave gaps by 2035. The next simulation (SIM-5) tests the comprehensive approach: moderate intake increase AND full environment reform.
