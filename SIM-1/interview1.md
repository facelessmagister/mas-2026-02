> what are you doing to retain our doctors?

[DG Mahathar Abd Wahab]
  **Retaining our doctors is no longer a nice‑to‑have, it’s a matter of
  survival—especially after the simulation showed us exactly how quickly
  the system can tip over.**    When the model ran the 2026‑2030
  scenario, we saw three pivotal breaking points:  1. **2027‑Q2 – “The
  Klang Collapse”:** a tertiary centre in Klang ran out of resident
  specialists for cardiology and orthopaedics; elective surgeries were
  postponed for weeks, patients started queuing in the car park, morale
  hit rock bottom.   2. **2028‑Q1 – “Sabah Burnout”:** a remote district
  hospital lost 40 % of its medical officers within six months because
  the on‑call load was 120 h/week and there was no clear career path.
  3. **2029‑Q4 – “Private‑Public Drain”:** the private sector offered
  30 % higher salaries plus a “fast‑track” specialist track; half of our
  junior consultants jumped ship, leaving the public system with a 25 %
  vacancy rate in core specialties.  Those numbers stopped me in my
  tracks. What we need now is a **whole‑system, end‑to‑end approach** –
  not just ad‑hoc cash bonuses. Here’s what I am doing, right now, and
  what I’m pushing for in the next 12‑18 months:  ---  ### 1. **Unified
  Health Services Commission (UHSC)** - **Why:** The simulation showed
  fragmented workforce planning – each ministry, each state, each
  hospital was playing its own game. The result? Over‑production of
  certain specialties and severe undersupply in others. - **What we’ve
  done:** I have drafted the *Health Services Commission Bill* and
  secured a provisional endorsement from the Ministry of Finance. The
  UHSC will own the *entire talent pipeline* – from medical school
  admission quotas to retirement planning.   - **Immediate impact:** A
  single data‑hub now feeds real‑time vacancy, attrition and
  training‑output numbers to the Commission, allowing us to
  **re‑allocate training slots within weeks**, not years.  ### 2.
  **Competency‑Based Training (CBT) & Career Ladders** - **Simulation
  insight:** Doctors left because they felt “stuck” in a generic
  residency; they wanted clear competencies that translated into
  recognisable, marketable skills. - **Action:** All postgraduate
  programmes are now being re‑designed around **competency clusters**
  (e.g., “Advanced Laparoscopic Surgery”, “Rural Emergency Care”).   -
  **Career ladder:** After every competency milestone, a doctor can
  **choose a pathway** – Academic, Clinical Specialist, or Rural Health
  Leader – each with its own salary band, research grant, and leadership
  training.   - **Result so far:** In the pilot at Hospital Selayang,
  78 % of junior doctors reported “greater sense of purpose”, and
  turnover dropped from 12 % to 5 % in six months.  ### 3. **Targeted
  Retention Packages – Not Just Money** | Issue | Retention Measure |
  How it works | |-------|-------------------|--------------| | **Rural
  posting fatigue** | **Rural Service Allowance + Housing Equity** |
  25 % salary top‑up *plus* a “home‑buy‑back” scheme: after five years
  of rural service, the government matches 50 % of the down‑payment on a
  house in the doctor’s hometown. | | **Workload overload** |
  **Protected “Clinical Hours”** | Caps on on‑call at 60 h/month; any
  excess must be covered by locum pool funded by the UHSC. | |
  **Professional development** | **Continuous Learning Stipend** |
  RM 2,500 per year per doctor for conferences, simulation labs, or
  e‑learning – fully reimbursed on receipt of attendance proof. | |
  **Mental health & well‑being** | **Doctor‑Wellness Hubs** | 24‑hour
  confidential counselling, peer‑support groups, and quarterly
  “Resilience Workshops” embedded in every hospital. |  These packages
  are **bundled**, not offered in isolation, because the simulation
  taught us that pulling a doctor out on salary alone is a *temporary
  fix* – the underlying stressors remain.  ### 4. **Task‑Sharing &
  Team‑Based Care** - **What we learned:** In 2028‑Sabah, the lack of
  junior doctors forced nurses to take on unsupervised prescribing,
  leading to errors and further burnout. - **Implementation:** We are
  rolling out *Advanced Practice Clinician* (APC) programmes for
